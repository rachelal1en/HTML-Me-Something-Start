Jewel at Riverport



August 18, 1999

This was my very first concert and I attended with my parents and brother. I don't remember the details but treasure the experience still.

Bad Beaver Punk Shows


2005

This was a hard time in my life and my favorite music was LOUD. Music I could feel in my chest like a heartbeat. The walls in the basement were wet with sweat, the crowd was packed in so tight, and we were all there to embrace the volume and anger in punk music. I was very blessed to find Bad Beaver as an outlet when I needed it.

Fête de la musique - Seven Nation Army



June 21, 2008

Wandering through Paris and exploring the different pockets of music was electric. It was right next to the Eiffel Tower where we decided to stop and observe. We learned from fellow festival goers how to pop out a wine cork with an elbow. We joined in the crowd near the speakers to celebrate and dance. When the opening chords of Seven Nation Army by the White Stripes played the crowd went wild and so did I. The oneness with the crowd was a religious experience.

Listener in my friend's basement



Summer 2010

The summer of 2010 was the most transitionary time in my life. Listener is essentially spoken-word in some of their songs. I was very into spoken-word at the time (and am still). The depth of feeling in their words deeply moved me and I still look back upon as something I really needed at that time.

Yeasayer at The Firebird



June 27, 2013

The Firebird was my favorite venue in St. Louis and I truly believe the city is worse for no longer having it. It was hard to pick which of the shows I saw there was my favorite but Yeasayer tops the list. The strong musical backing that complements the lyrics pound through the crowd like a heartbeat.

Third Eye Blind



July 11, 2017

My friend Kayla was in town from medical school with her friend Cat. While they were out at the Crack Fox, they met the drummer from Third Eye Blind and got VIP tickets for the 3 of us to their performance as well as his number. We have very few pictures from that night because we were late and will call was closed by the time we arrived. My friend from middle school Stephanie was working the gate and I was able to talk her into letting us in anyway. So, we made it in but obviously didn't know where our seats in VIP were supposed to be nor had proof we were even allowed in that section. We hung out in the aisles for the end of the performance and then texted him that we were here but didn't know where to go for the after party. He told us where to go and told security to let us in and it was us and a few other girls on blow-up couches with a cooler of beer. It was such an odd but fun and interesting night. We hung out with them all night until like 2am when their tour bus was leaving to take-off to their next destination. All the gates were closed so we had to wander around to try to get out and then also try to escape the parking lot with closed gates. It was a crazy night.

Death Cab for Cutie at the Factory



October 13, 2022

This was my second time seeing Death Cab live but this show was much different than my first experience because it was after the profound loss of who I attended with the previous time.

Fleetwood Mac Laser Show



2023 & 2024

My mom is a member of the St. Louis Science Center and pays attention to their events to utilize it. When she saw laser shows based upon music advertised to take place in the Planetarium last year, I wasn't sure what to expect. She and I attended "Laser Zepplin" which was okay but afterward I knew it would be phenomenal with Fleetwood Mac's discography. It was a transcendent experience to be laying on the ground (we brought pillows and blankets) and being completely immersed in the music while watching the lasers on the curved ceiling. I jumped at the opportunity to attend again this year with more of my friends, I would recommend more people to attend but there are so few Fleetwood Mac themed shows and I don't want the tickets to become even harder to get.

The Eras Tour Movie/Streaming



2023-2024

I, unfortunately, was not able to get tickets to see The Eras Tour live. I am very grateful for the people that livestream them while they are there and for Tess on TikTok who assembles them to be a mostly continuous stream. Also, seeing the tour in the movie theater production was a lot of fun since it has the theater sound system. I have enjoyed watching to see what kind of Suprise songs/mashups occur and being a part of the community that celebrates and theorizes about the performances.
